F1 In Schools

F1 in Schools is a worldwide STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics) competition for elementary and high school students comprising of more than 20,000,000 participants from over 44 different countries around the world, who work together and collaborate with experts to design, modulate, test, develop, assemble and race a micrographic of an F1 racing car. The participants is not only to design the car and obtain sponsorships to cover the costs, but are also required to advertise their car to the public. Needless to say, this competition entails sufficient time-management skills, hard-working and determined participants willing to step out of their comfort zone. F1 in Schools is the only global multi-disciplinary challenge in which teams of students aged 9 to 19 deploy CAD/CAM software to collaborate, design, analyze, manufacture, test, and then race miniature compressed air powered cars made from F1 model blocks.
